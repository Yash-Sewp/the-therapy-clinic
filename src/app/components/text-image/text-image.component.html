Occupational Therapy

Occupational therapy is a client-centred health profession concerned with promoting the health and well-being of individuals, in the roles that they need to perform. The primary goal of therapy is to enable people to participate in the activities of everyday life. Occupational therapists achieve this outcome by working with people and communities to enhance their ability to engage in the occupations (play, school, work, roles) they want to, need to, or are expected to do. If rendered unable to, then modification of the occupation or the environment is implemented to better support their occupational engagement.

What Does Occupational Therapy Have To Do With My Child?

Your child’s life is made up of occupations, or everyday activities. These occupations include playing, learning, sleeping and resting, interacting with friends, getting dressed, and other daily activities. Many of us generally don’t think about a child’s daily occupations until he or she has challenges doing them. Everyone has occupations—from the toddler whose occupations are playing and learning, to the older child whose occupations are being a student and developing the skills to become more independent.

Speech & Hearing Therapy

Speech therapy includes articulation therapy (difficulty with pronunciation of sounds), as well as language development and intervention activities depending on the type of speech or language disorder. Speech language therapists also assess and manage difficulties with the processing of sounds and the skills needed to develop literacy and comprehension.

Trauma Counselling

Trauma counselling is a short-term intervention, which is indicated when a person has suffered a traumatic incident. The therapist provides a grounded presence where you can begin to explore your trauma while feeling safe, listened to and held. Counselling is the skilled and principled use of relationship to facilitate self- knowledge, emotional acceptance and growth and the optimal development of personal resources. The overall aim is to provide an opportunity to work towards living more satisfyingly and resourcefully. Counselling relationships will vary according to need but may be concerned with developmental issues, addressing and resolving specific problems, making decisions, coping with crisis, developing personal insights and knowledge, working through feelings of inner conflict or improving relationships with others.
